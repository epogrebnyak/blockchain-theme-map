# Blockchain and Digital Assets Theme Map

*Last revision: July 21, 2026*

Source: <https://github.com/epogrebnyak/blockchain-theme-map>


## USE CASES

*What blockchain is useful for?*


### What users need blockchain for?


**Speculative**

- Spot trading (BTC, ETH, altcoins)
- Memecoins
- Prediction markets

**Institutional finance (CeFi)**

- Payments
- Exchange trading
- Custody and prime brokerage
- RWA issuance and trading
- Derivatives

**Decentralised finance (DeFi)**

- Staking (Lido, Rocket Pool)
- Collateralised lending (Aave, Compound)
- Exchange/AMM (Uniswap, Curve)
- Derivatives (dYdX, GMX, perpetuals)
- RWA protocols (MakerDAO, Centrifuge)

**Physical**

- Wireless networks (Helium)
- Storage (Filecoin, Arweave)
- Compute (Render, Akash)
- Supply chain and provenance

**Social and governance**

- Identity
- Reputation and credentials
- Voting rights, DAOs

**Entertainment**

- Gaming
- Collectibles


## MINIMAL BLOCKCHAIN

*Core building blocks of blockchain systems*


### Transactions


**Create**

- Native issuance (block rewards, staking and validation)
- Token minting
- Airdrops and rewards

**Acquire**

- At the table or P2P
- Broker, ATM or card
- Exchanges

**Exchange**

- Centralised exchanges (CEX)
- Decentralised protocols (DEX)
- Aggregators
- Solvers and intent-based execution
- Cross-chain bridges


### Assets and contract types

- Native currency (BTC, ETH, SOL)
- Stablecoins (USDC, USDT, DAI, USDe)
- Other fungible tokens (ERC-20, SPL)
- Non-fungible tokens (NFTs)
- Semi-fungible tokens
- Soulbound tokens (non-transferable)
- Off-chain data (oracles, storage proofs)


### Incentives

- Fee models (gas and priority fees)
- MEV (order flow, arbitrage and liquidations)
- Staking yields and rewards
- Token supply and burn


### Networks

- Bitcoin (BTC)
- Ethereum (ETH)
- L2 and L3 Rollups
- Alternative L1s (BNB, Solana, Tron)
- Private and consortium chains


### Foundations

- Cryptography (hashing, digital signatures and Merkle trees)
- Address space and block data structures
- Peer-to-peer networking (node discovery and gossip protocols)
- Consensus mechanisms (participants, rules and guarantees)
- Execution environments (VMs and state transitions)
- API and user interface, account and wallet abstraction


## USER ACCESS AND CUSTODY

*How users and institutions hold and access assets*


### Wallets and key management

- Self-custodial wallets (MetaMask, Phantom, Trust Wallet)
- Smart contract wallets (Safe, Argent, Sequence)
- Account abstraction (ERC-4337, ERC-7579)
- Paymasters (gas abstraction, sponsored transactions)
- Hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor)
- Key recovery and social recovery


### Institutional custody

- MPC wallets (Fireblocks, ZenGo)
- Multi-sig custody solutions
- Qualified custodians (Coinbase Custody, BitGo)
- Prime brokerage and settlement


## NETWORK ENHANCEMENTS

*Components that expand blockchain capabilities*


### Interoperability

- Bridges
- Cross-chain messaging
- Light clients


### Oracles and data feeds

- Price feeds (Chainlink)
- Randomness
- Cross-chain data
- Real-world event data (sports, weather and elections)
- Data aggregation and verification


### Storage

- On-chain storage (state and balances)
- Off-chain storage (IPFS, Arweave and Filecoin)
- Data availability layers (Celestia and EigenDA)
- Decentralized databases (Ceramic and OrbitDB)


## FEATURES AND PROTECTION

*Privacy, safeguards and compliance controls*


### Privacy and user protection

- Zero-knowledge proofs (ZK-SNARKs and ZK-STARKs)
- Stealth addresses
- Confidential transactions (hidden amounts)
- Private voting
- Encrypted metadata
- Selective disclosure (ZK-based KYC/age verification)


### Contract security

- Smart contract audits
- Multi-sig and custody
- Bug bounties
- Formal verification
- MEV protection


### Compliance

- KYC and AML
- Sanctions screening
- Transfer restrictions
- Travel rule
- Reporting and tax


## RISK MANAGEMENT

*Lessons and persistent risks across ecosystems*


### Past incidents


**CeFi failures**

- Mt. Gox (2014)
- FTX (2022), Celsius (2022), BlockFi (2022)

**DeFi exploits**

- The DAO (2016), Poly Network (2021), Ronin Bridge (2022), Euler Finance (2023)

**Stablecoin collapses**

- Terra/LUNA (2022), USDC de-peg (2023)

**Speculative waves**

- ICO boom (2017-2018)
- NFT bubble (2021-2022)

**Regulatory actions**

- Binance (2023-2024)
- Tornado Cash (2022)
