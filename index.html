Persistent threats

Risk categories
Risk category Description Example or notes
Algorithmic risks Mathematical bugs in consensus, bonding curves, or AMM formulas Terra/LUNA's death spiral was algorithmic, not a hack
Oracle manipulation Price feed manipulation via flash loans or low-liquidity pools Mango Markets (2022) - exploited via manipulated oracle prices
Bridge and interoperability risks Cross-chain messages can be spoofed or replayed Any bridge with a trusted validator set is a target
Smart contract vulnerabilities Re-entrancy, access control, logic bugs, upgradeability risks The DAO (2016), still relevant today
Key management and custody risks Private key loss, theft, or compromise Social engineering, phishing, sim-swapping, insider threats
Governance risks Token-based voting can be captured by whales or attackers Aave, Compound - governance attacks remain theoretical but persistent
Behavioral and social engineering Honey-pots, rug pulls, fake websites, malicious browser extensions Pump-and-dump schemes, influencer manipulation
Systemic contagion Concentration of stablecoin reserves in a single bank (e.g., USDC/SVB) Interconnected leverage across DeFi protocols
