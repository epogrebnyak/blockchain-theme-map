Layer 6 Risk Management
Lessons learned
Past incidents
6 groups
CeFi failures
  • Mt. Gox (2014)
  • FTX (2022)
  • Celsius (2022)
  • BlockFi (2022)
DeFi exploits
  • The DAO (2016) - Re-entrancy, led to Ethereum fork
  • Poly Network (2021) - Bridge exploit ($611M)
  • Ronin Bridge (2022) - Private key compromise ($625M)
  • Euler Finance (2023) - Flash loan attack ($197M)
Stablecoin collapses
  • Terra/LUNA (2022) - Algorithmic death spiral ($40B)
  • USDC temporary de-peg (2023) - Silicon Valley Bank exposure
Speculative waves
  • ICO boom (2017-2018) - Scams, retail losses, regulatory backlash
  • NFT bubble (2021-2022) - Wash trading, illiquidity
Regulatory actions
  • Binance (2023-2024) - DOJ charges, regulatory settlements
  • Tornado Cash (2022) - OFAC sanctions on smart contracts
Key takeaways
  • Centralized custody introduces counterparty risk
  • Bridges remain the largest attack surface
  • Algorithmic stability without collateral is fragile
  • Regulatory clarity is essential for institutional adoption
  • Code is law fails when governance is weak
Persistent threats
Risk categories
8 categories
Algorithmic risks
  • Mathematical bugs in consensus, bonding curves, or AMM formulas
  • Example: Terra/LUNA's death spiral was algorithmic, not a hack
Oracle manipulation
  • Price feed manipulation via flash loans or low-liquidity pools
  • Example: Mango Markets (2022) - exploited via manipulated oracle prices
Bridge and interoperability risks
  • Cross-chain messages can be spoofed or replayed
  • Example: Any bridge with a trusted validator set is a target
Smart contract vulnerabilities
  • Re-entrancy, access control, logic bugs, upgradeability risks
  • Example: The DAO (2016), still relevant today
Key management and custody risks
  • Private key loss, theft, or compromise
  • Social engineering, phishing, sim-swapping, insider threats
Governance risks
  • Token-based voting can be captured by whales or attackers
  • Example: Aave, Compound - governance attacks remain theoretical but persistent
Behavioral and social engineering
  • Honey-pots, rug pulls, fake websites, malicious browser extensions
  • Pump-and-dump schemes, influencer manipulation
Systemic contagion
  • Concentration of stablecoin reserves in a single bank (e.g., USDC/SVB)
  • Interconnected leverage across DeFi protocols
